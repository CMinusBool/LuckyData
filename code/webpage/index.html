Cyclists in Taipei

A data-informed approach to understanding and improving urban cycling experiences

Project Overview

Cycling in Taipei is growing - but for many, it still doesn’t feel safe. Let’s explore together how we can make biking in this city safer and more accessible by understanding what everyday cyclists face and what really needs to change. We spent time in busy areas like Taipei Main Station to take a close look at road conditions and cyclist behavior. Then we came together to talk about the most common challenges - and safety kept coming up. By combining these personal insights with our observations and supported by data from the government, this project offers a data-informed perspective on how to improve cycling conditions in Taipei - from better infrastructure planning to raising awareness about safe riding practices.

Safety Perception

How safe do cyclists feel in Taipei?

Many riders told us they feel unsafe, especially where bike lanes are missing or unclear. Our survey confirmed this: a large number of cyclists worry about traffic, road layout, and the lack of safe space. The average safety perception rating among all surveyed cyclists was:

Critical Findings

Our analysis of the Taipei City Road Traffic Accident data reveals distinct patterns in cycling-related incidents across the city. The highest concentration of accidents occurs at major transit hubs and commercial districts, particularly during rush hours.

Complex Intersections

Multi-directional traffic flows with inadequate cycling infrastructure create confusion and risk

Commercial Districts

High pedestrian activity, delivery vehicles, and limited space increase conflict potential

Peak Hours

Evening rush hour (5-7 PM) shows significantly higher accident rates across all districts

Conclusions & Recommendations

Our research reveals a clear need for improved cycling infrastructure in Taipei, particularly in high-traffic urban areas. While Taipei has made progress with YouBike stations and riverside paths, there remains a significant gap in safe urban cycling routes.

01

Infrastructure Expansion

Expand dedicated bike lane network in central districts, particularly along major commuting corridors

02

Safety Signage

Improve visibility and signage at high-risk intersections to alert both cyclists and motorists

03

Navigation Application

Develop a safety-focused navigation app that incorporates real-time hazard alerts and safer route recommendations

04

Educational Campaigns

Create targeted campaigns on safe cycling practices for both locals and international residents

05

Physical Barriers

Install physical separation between bike lanes and vehicle traffic on major roads to enhance safety

These data-informed recommendations could significantly improve the cycling experience in Taipei and support the city's sustainability goals.

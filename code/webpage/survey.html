Survey Data Visualization

Interactive data visualizations based on our cycling survey, which received 38 responses in total.

How often do people ride bikes in Taipei?


Most people ride bikes either daily, weekly, or rarely, with 10 respondents for each. Seven people ride bikes monthly, and only one person said they never ride. This shows that biking is a common activity in Taipei, but the frequency is different for each person.

Main reasons for riding a bicycle


Most people ride bicycles for commuting, with 24 respondents choosing this reason. Other popular reasons are for fun (7 responses) and exercise (5 responses). Only one person rides a bicycle to attend exhibitions, and one person selected “all of the above.” This shows that most bicycle use in Taipei is for practical travel, while some people also enjoy riding for leisure and health.

Safety perception while cycling in Taipei


13 participants rated their cycling safety as level 4, showing they feel quite safe. 9 participants chose level 3, meaning they feel neutral. 7 participants selected level 5, showing they feel very safe. However, 6 participants chose level 2, and 3 participants rated it as level 1, which means they feel unsafe. This shows that while many cyclists feel safe, some still have safety concerns in Taipei.

Biggest concerns when cycling


The most common concern is not enough bike lanes, with 14 participants choosing this. Reckless drivers is the second biggest concern, selected by 12 participants. Traffic congestion was chosen by 6 participants. Only 2 participants were concerned about poor road conditions and another 2 about poor visibility at night. One participant selected all of the above, and one mentioned pedestrians on bike lanes. This shows that most cyclists in Taipei are worried about safety and road design.

Suggested improvements for cycling safety


The most common suggestion was more bike lanes, with 19 participants choosing this. More traffic signs for cyclists was chosen by 7 participants, and better road maintenance was suggested by 6 participants. Stricter traffic laws received 5 responses, and only 1 participant suggested that pedestrians should get off the bike lanes. This shows that most cyclists believe increasing bike lanes is the best way to improve safety in Taipei

Would use a safety navigation app?


22 participants said Yes, they would use a safety navigation app. 14 participants answered Maybe, showing they are interested but not fully sure. Only 2 participants said No. This means most people are open to using technology to make cycling safer in Taipei.

Age Groups


Most participants were between 25–34 years old (19 people). The second largest group was 18–24 years old (16 people). A smaller number of participants were aged 35–44 (3 people) and 45–54 (1 person). This shows the survey mostly reached young adults.

Gender


The majority of participants were female (22 people), followed by male (15 people). Only 1 person identified as other. This shows that more women joined the survey than men.

Origin


Most respondents were international (21 people). The rest came from Taipei (10 people) and other Taiwanese cities (7 people). This means the survey included both local and international cyclists, with more responses from foreigners.

About this project

This project was developed as part of a research seminar Data-informed Design Thinking, supervised by Ching-I Chen, in the spring semester 2025 at National Taipei University of Technology.

Methods

To explore the current state of bicycle infrastructure and perceptions of cycling safety in Taipei, we employed a mixed-methods approach combining data analysis, visualization, and user input.
First, we obtained spatial data on existing bike lanes from the City Science Lab. Initial visualizations were created using Kepler.gl to identify patterns and gaps in the bike lane network. To gain a deeper understanding of cyclists' experiences and perceptions, we conducted a survey titled "Exploring the Impact of Missing Bike Lanes on Urban Mobility and Safety" using Google Forms. The survey results provided valuable insights into how the absence of bike lanes affects the feeling of safety and overall mobility for cyclists in Taipei.
Building on these findings, along with additional independent research, we developed the content presented on this website. The first version of the site was generated using the AI-powered development tool Cursor and then refined and edited by our team.

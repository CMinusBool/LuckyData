Cyclists in Taipei

Safe Ride or Bold Adventure? A Data-Informed Journey

Project Overview

Cycling in Taipei is growing - but for many, it still doesn't feel safe. Let's explore together how we can make biking in this city safer and more accessible by understanding what everyday cyclists face and what really needs to change. We spent time in busy areas like Taipei Main Station to take a close look at road conditions and cyclist behavior. Then we came together to talk about the most common challenges - and safety kept coming up. By combining these personal insights with our observations and supported by data from the government, this project offers a data-informed perspective on how to improve cycling conditions in Taipei - from better infrastructure planning to raising awareness about safe riding practices.

Safety Perception

How safe do cyclists feel in Taipei?

Many riders told us they feel unsafe, especially where bike lanes are missing or unclear. Our survey confirmed this: a large number of cyclists worry about traffic, road layout, and the lack of safe space. The average safety perception rating among all surveyed cyclists was:

Recommendations

To make cycling in Taipei safer and more enjoyable, we propose the following data-informed recommendations:

Expand and improve bike lane infrastructure

Creating more protected bike lanes can help reduce accidents and make cyclists feel safer and more comfortable on the road.

Install clearer and more cyclist-specific traffic signage

Proper signs can improve the navigation for the cyclists to find the correct way and remind all road users to share the road.

Enhance road surface conditions

Improving the road quality by repairing uneven or damaged surfaces can help prevent accidents and make cycling smoother and safer.

Strengthen traffic law enforcement

Implementing and enforcing stricter traffic regulations can deter reckless driving and offer greater protection for cyclists.

Provide accessible information on safe cycling routes

Sharing maps and route recommendations can help cyclists avoid dangerous areas and navigate the city more safely.

Promote road-sharing awareness and education

Public education campaigns can help drivers, pedestrians, and cyclists understand traffic rules and respect bike lanes.

These data-informed recommendations could significantly improve the cycling experience in Taipei and support the city's sustainability goals.

Conclusion

This research explored the experiences and safety concerns of bicycle users in Taipei. Most people ride bicycles for commuting, and YouBike is the most common type. Although few participants reported accidents, many shared concerns such as the lack of bike lanes, reckless drivers, and traffic congestion. Most riders felt only moderately safe while cycling.

Findings from related studies support these concerns. Higher speeds and poor road design increase the risk of crashes and cause mental stress. Many cyclists also do not wear helmets, especially those using shared bikes, which raises the chance of serious injury during an accident.

To improve biking safety and access, it is important to listen to everyday cyclists and understand what they face. Taipei should build more protected bike lanes, fix road conditions, add clear signs, and promote helmet use. Education campaigns and stricter traffic law enforcement can also help all road users respect cycling rules. These actions will make biking safer, more comfortable, and more reliable in the city.
